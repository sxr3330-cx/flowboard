Built for the round
Every feature in Flowboard was designed around a specific debate moment. Here's what you get.
⚡
Real-Time Sync
Both partners see every change the moment it's typed. Firebase-powered sub-second latency from any device, anywhere in the world.
📋
Flow Sheets per Position
One sheet per position — Advantages, DAs, Ks, CPs. Add a "Politics DA" and it opens at 1NC. Add "Advantage 1" and it opens at 1AC.
↩
Respond System
Click ↩ on any arg and a linked response card appears in the next speech column. The flow scrolls and focuses it automatically.
📝
Live Speech Doc
Shared speech panel with Flow Access mode — click any argument to pull it into your speech. Dropped args insert "The [side] dropped:" automatically.
🔍
Evidence Search
Search open-source debate evidence from OpenEv and OpenCaselist. Upload your HSPolicy 25-26 cards and search them natively inside the flow.
↑
Extend to Speech
Click ↑ on any argument and choose which speech to extend it to. A copy is created in the target speech, marked EXT, with your notes intact.
⊠
Kick Entire Flows
Kick a position (Cap K, Politics DA) with one click from the sidebar. The entire flow sheet is crossed out for both partners instantly.
⇄
Cross-Apply Arguments
Link arguments across speeches and flows. Cross-apply badges show connections and clicking them scrolls to the linked argument instantly.
👁
Spectator Mode
Room creator controls who can edit. Spectators see the full flow live but can't make changes. Toggle editor/spectator access from the presence row.
↩
Undo / Redo
Full undo/redo history with Ctrl+Z and Ctrl+Shift+Z. Every add, delete, and status change is undoable without leaving the flow.

Persistent Rooms

Our story
Built by debaters,
for debaters
Flowboard was born from the frustration of losing rounds because of a dropped argument on a messy paper flow.
Every policy debater knows the feeling. You're in the 2AR, you know the neg dropped the agriculture solvency extension in the 1NR, but your paper flow is three pages of scribble and your partner is pointing at a different column. You go for it anyway. The judge doesn't buy it because you can't explain where it was extended.

"The flow is the round. If you can't read it, you've already lost."

Flowboard is the platform we wished existed when we were prepping for tournaments. Real-time shared flows, a speech doc that builds itself from what you click, and a card search that actually works. No account required, no setup, no friction — just open a room, share the code, and flow.
The respond system was the first thing we built. Nothing is more frustrating than trying to track which NC arg you're responding to in the 2AC. Now you click ↩, a box appears in the right column, and it's linked. The kick feature came from watching neg teams forget they read the cap K in the 1NC. The speech doc came from watching 2ARs that got lost because the debater was reading off paper instead of the flow.
