Our story

Built by debaters who got tired of losing rounds to illegible paper.

Flowboard started as a frustration. It's now the flow platform for competitive Policy, LD, and PF.

Every policy debater knows the feeling. You're in the 2AR. You know the neg dropped the agriculture solvency extension in the 1NR. Your paper flow is three pages of scribble. Your partner is pointing at a different column. You go for it anyway. The judge doesn't buy it — not because you were wrong, but because you couldn't prove you were right.

"The flow is the round. If you can't read it, you've already lost."

Flowboard is the platform we wished existed during prep. Real-time shared flows, a speech doc that builds from what you click, and card search that lives inside the tool. No account required. No setup. Open a room, share the code, and flow.
The respond system came first — the single most frustrating thing in debate is trying to track which NC argument you're responding to in the 2AC. Now you click respond on a 1AC arg and a linked card appears in the 1NC. The kick feature came from watching neg teams forget they read the cap K in the 1NC. The speech doc came from watching 2ARs fall apart because the debater was reading off paper instead of the flow.

What we believe

Our values

01
Speed is everything in round
Every millisecond of latency costs you. We use Firebase Realtime Database for sub-second sync because in a cross-examination, every second you spend hunting for an arg is a second your opponent uses to reframe the round.
02
The flow is the round
Judges vote on what's in the flow, not what you said. Flowboard is built around this reality — every feature exists to make your flow clearer, more organized, and more accurate than your opponent's.
03
Both partners need the same information
Half of all debate losses come from miscommunication between partners. When one person extends an argument the other thought was kicked, the round falls apart. Real-time shared flows eliminate this entirely.
04
Free access is non-negotiable
Debate is already inaccessible enough — expensive camps, expensive travel, expensive coaches. Flowboard will never put core features behind a paywall. If you're a debater, you deserve the same tools as anyone else.
How we got here

The Flowboard timeline

01
Early 2025
The first flow
A simple shared Google Doc was the original Flowboard. Two debaters, one doc, no structure. Arguments got lost in real-time just like they did on paper — but at least both partners could see the same thing.
02
Mid 2025
The respond button
The first real feature: a linked response system. Click respond on a 1AC argument and a connected card opens in the 1NC column. This single feature changed how we thought about flowing entirely.
03
Late 2025
Flow sheets and real-time sync
Firebase Realtime Database replaced the Google Doc backend. Flow sheets — one per position, starting at the right speech — replaced the single shared document. The platform started to look like what Flowboard is today.
04
2026
Flowboard goes public
Speech doc, card search, spectator mode, kick flows, cross-apply, undo/redo, Google sign-in, and an interactive globe showing where debaters are flowing from around the world. Flowboard is now the platform we always wanted.
